Operational environment:

The bank is located in a coastal area with low crime rates. Many people and systems handle the bank's data—100 on-premise employees and 20 remote employees. The customer base of the bank includes 2,000 individual accounts and 200 commercial accounts. The bank's services are marketed by a professional sports team and ten local businesses in the community. There are strict financial regulations that require the bank to secure their data and funds, like having enough cash available each day to meet Federal Reserve requirements.

Notes

How are security events possible considering the risks the asset faces in its operating environment?

Business email compromise: The confidential information shared by the employee can be exploited by a threat actor to damage financial resources or business reputation. The likelihood of an attack after being tricked into sharing confidential information through the business email is moderate. BEC attack can lead to a leak of personal data of the employees and other confidential records of the bank which is a moderate severity.

Compromised user database: Poorly encrypted customer data can cause a leak of personal or financial information of the customers in the event of a cyber attack. On top of that, doing business with other companies might increase the risks to data since it presents other avenues for the information to be compromised. The chance of an attack on a poorly encrypted database is pretty likely. The severity is high as it can damage business reputation and can lead to financial losses and regulatory charges.

Financial records leak: The database server of backed up data that is publicly accessible can be exploited by a threat actor at any time and is a huge vulnerability. The information stored in that database is at high risk. It is likely that a threat actor can act on it. The implications of leaked financial records are catastrophic.

Theft: An unlocked safe is an invitation to threat actors. Any asset stored in that safe is vulnerable and is at high risk. It can affect the cash reserve requirements set by the Federal Reserve which can cause non-compliance issues, leading to fines and other regulatory charges. So the risk of theft is important. However, it might not be as likely as the bank is in an area with low crime rates.

Supply chain disruption: Delivery delays due to natural disasters can cause untimely transfer of essential financial information or assets to the bank’s stakeholders. Whilst the severity of a supply chain disruption can affect the bank’s reputation and assets, the likelihood of a natural disaster occurring is pretty low.

Asset: The asset at risk of being harmed, damaged, or stolen.

Risk(s): A potential risk to the organization's information systems and data.

Description: A vulnerability that might lead to a security incident.

Likelihood: Score from 1-3 of the chances of a vulnerability being exploited. A 1 means there's a low likelihood, a 2 means there's a moderate likelihood, and a 3 means there's a high likelihood.

Severity: Score from 1-3 of the potential damage the threat would cause to the business. A 1 means a low severity impact, a 2 is a moderate severity impact, and a 3 is a high severity impact.

Priority: How quickly a risk should be addressed to avoid the potential incident. Use the following formula to calculate the overall score: Likelihood x Impact Severity = Risk

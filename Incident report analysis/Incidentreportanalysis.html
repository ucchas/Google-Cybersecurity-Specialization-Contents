Summary

A DDoS attack was experienced by the company recently. The internal network of the company was compromised for two hours before the cybersecurity team acted on it.

Identify

The incident management team analyzed the issue. It was detected to be an ICMP flood attack that rendered the internal network suddenly obsolete. A malicious actor had sent a flood of ICMP pings into the company’s network through an unconfigured firewall. This incident allowed the malicious attacker to overwhelm the network through a DDoS(Distributed Denial of Service) attack.

Protect

To detect abnormal traffic patterns in the future, network monitoring software is installed by the network security team. Also, to prevent these kinds of attacks password policies need to be implemented system-wide. Implementation of Multi-Factor Authentication(MFA), suspension of activity after 3 failed login attempts, and training all employees on how to protect their password is greatly needed to improve authentication.

Detect

To stop these kinds of attacks in the future the network security team implemented a new firewall rule to limit the rate of incoming ICMP packets. The firewall will also verify the source IP address to check for spoofed IP addresses on incoming ICMP packets. An IDS/IPS system is also implemented to filter out some suspicious ICMP traffic.

Respond

The incident management team responded by blocking incoming ICMP packets and stopping all non-critical network services offline. On top of that, training shall be provided to all employees regarding the protection of their login credentials. Management should be informed of the incident. Management should inform affected employees, customers, and appropriate law enforcement agencies about this incident.

Recover

The incident management team has restored all critical network services. The customer information violated due to this incident shall be communicated with the concerned customers and staff to be rectified as soon as possible.
